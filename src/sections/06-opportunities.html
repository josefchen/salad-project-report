Know who's leaving

The churn model scores every returning customer after their 2nd visit. The top 5% highest-risk customers can be flagged for personal attention — the model finds roughly twice as many actual leavers as picking at random. Use this to focus staff effort where it matters most.

Ready now

Convert till-only customers to accounts

82% of trackable customers are till-only — we can score their risk but can't reach them by email or phone. Prompting account sign-up at the till (or via QR on receipts) turns them into contactable customers, unlocking direct follow-up for future retention.

Quick win
📍

Investigate high-churn stores

Churn rates vary significantly between locations. Stores with unusually high churn may have experience problems (slow service, quality inconsistency) rather than a marketing problem. Compare the worst-performing stores against the best to find what's different.

Quick win
🔄

Get the second visit

The biggest drop-off is between order 1 and order 2. A small nudge within the first week — a discount, a thank-you message, a reason to come back — could shift a meaningful number of one-timers into repeat customers. This only works for the 18% on digital channels; for till-only customers, it has to happen in-store.
